## Web research

### Search 1: 2020 Pivot Firebird 29 common issues / reliability
- **Fox Float X2 squelch is a recurring, well-documented issue** across
  multiple Firebird 29 generations (and across Fox X2-equipped bikes
  generally). One reviewer reports £300+ across 3 rebuilds in 3 years
  before swapping the shock entirely. The seller's "rear shock due for
  routine rebuild" disclosure is consistent with this issue and worth
  budgeting for either a rebuild or a shock swap.
  Sources: [Singletrack 2023 review](https://singletrackworld.com/2023/04/pivot-firebird-29-ride-slx-xt-air-review/),
  [MBR Firebird 29 thread](https://www.mtbr.com/threads/firebird-29.1080876/page-32)
- **First-gen (2018–2021) Firebird 29 has a yoke-clevis design that
  damages coil shocks** — Pivot's warranty specifically excludes most
  coil-shock failures. Not an issue for this listing (still has the
  OE Float X2) but matters if a future buyer planned to swap to coil.
  Source: [MBR forum](https://www.mtbr.com/threads/firebird-29.1080876/page-32)
- **Bearing-seal complaint applies to the 2nd-gen (2022+) Firebird,
  not this 2020.** Don't transfer that finding to this listing — the
  first-gen frame is a different design.
  Source: [Singletrack 2023 review](https://singletrackworld.com/2023/04/pivot-firebird-29-ride-slx-xt-air-review/)

### Search 2: 2020 Pivot Firebird 29 review (geometry, ride, build)
- **63.9° head angle, 471mm reach (size L), 431mm chainstays, 347mm BB.**
  Slack but not extreme; agile rear end, demands an active rider for
  front-grip on flat corners. Reviewers consistently call it "hellishly
  fast" on descents and "sluggish climber" with a slack-ish STA (75–77°
  effective vs. 78–79° on newer enduros).
  Sources: [MBR 2020 Pro review](https://www.mbr.co.uk/reviews/full-suspension-bikes/pivot-firebird-29-pro),
  [ENDURO Magazine](https://enduro-mtb.com/en/pivot-firebird-29-review/),
  [Blister 2018](https://blisterreview.com/gear-reviews/mountain-bike-reviews/2018-pivot-firebird-29)
- **Spec on this listing matches what reviewers liked.** Maxxis DHF/DHR
  II combo is the right tire for the bike's character; X01 Eagle and
  Fox Factory suspension are the trims reviewers tested favorably.
- **Build-kit value criticism on Pivot is real.** MTBR forum users
  complain the brand's complete builds are overpriced for the parts
  spec — but this is a *new-MSRP* observation. On the used market, the
  same brand's premium-spec retention works in the buyer's favor.
  Source: [MBR forum](https://www.mtbr.com/threads/pivot-firebird-29.1080892/)
