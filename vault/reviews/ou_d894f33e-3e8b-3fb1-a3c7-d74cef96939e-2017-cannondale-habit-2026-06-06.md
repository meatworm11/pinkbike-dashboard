## Web research

### Search 1: Cannondale Habit alloy 27.5 Zero Pivot common problems / reliability
- Early **2016 Bad Habit alloy owners reported cracked frames**; open question whether 2017 fixed it. Rear-pivot **creaking and dry/under-lubed linkage bearings** reported, especially after wet rides. The "engineered flex" Zero-Pivot stay drew skepticism on alloy frames.
- Sources: [MTBR — Habit reliability](https://forums.mtbr.com/cannondale/habit-reliability-1050005.html), [MTBR — Cannondale Habit thread](https://www.mtbr.com/threads/cannondale-habit.981004/page-22)

- Geometry is sound for an older trail bike (65.5° HTA, ~455mm reach M, 120mm rear), but reviewers consistently flag the **components as the bike's undoing** — "good geo, not so good components." 2017 Habit 4 ran a Revelation fork + Monarch RT + Deore brakes; the Alloy 5/6 below it are cheaper still.
- Sources: [Pinkbike — Value Field Test: Habit 4, good geo not so good components](https://www.pinkbike.com/news/value-field-test-cannondale-habit-4-good-geo-not-so-good-components.html), [MBR — Habit 5 (2017) review](https://www.mbr.co.uk/reviews/full-suspension-bikes/cannondale-habit-5), [REI — 2017 Habit Alloy 6](https://www.rei.com/product/109139/cannondale-habit-alloy-6-275-bike-2017)
