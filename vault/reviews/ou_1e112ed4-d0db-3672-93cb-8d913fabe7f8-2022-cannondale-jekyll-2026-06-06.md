## Web research

### Search 1: 2022 Cannondale Jekyll common problems reliability
- Stock alloy wheelset (WTB KOM Trail) is the most-cited weakness — spokes
  lose tension after a few rides and rims ding/knock out of true on
  impact. (Mitigated here *if* the ENVE/I9 upgrade is real.)
- High-pivot "Gravity Cavity" can ingest rocks/debris into the shock
  cavity; reviewers advise stopping at any rattling sound to avoid
  expensive shock damage (moto-foam fix exists).
- One long-term owner reported loose rear-suspension bearings, a "dead
  crusty" headset, a derailleur-hanger bolt breaking 3× and two
  derailleurs failing within ~9 months — inspect bearings, headset, and
  hanger.
- Internal cables rattle at the upper port (cosmetic/tape fix).

### Search 2: 2022 Cannondale Jekyll review
- 165mm rear / 170mm fork, 29" wheels; high-pivot Horst-link layout with
  an idler pulley and integrated upper chain guide; "Gravity Cavity" split
  downtube lets the shock sit very low.
- Reviews positive overall: fast, fun, intuitive, smooth and composed in
  rough terrain with well-supported pedaling; standout color-shift paint.
- Noted negatives: audible idler drag on climbs and debris-ingress risk in
  the Gravity Cavity.
