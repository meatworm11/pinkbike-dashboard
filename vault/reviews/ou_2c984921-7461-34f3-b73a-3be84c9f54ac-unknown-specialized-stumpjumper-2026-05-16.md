## Web research

### Search 1: Specialized Stumpjumper common issues reliability forum

- Rear pivot debris accumulation (rocks from tire) found on 2019 models;
  check that area for frame gouging on inspection.
- Creaking is a recurring complaint (BB and seatpost are primary culprits on
  alloy builds); plan for a BB service if creaking is present.
- Shock frame-interface wear noted on some builds — inspect shock eyelet area.
- Brake noise / rear brake shudder reported across multiple model years on
  some builds; check pads and rotor alignment.
- Pedal strikes are an acknowledged Stumpjumper design trait (intentionally
  low BB for cornering stability) — not a defect, but worth knowing.
- Sources: [MTBR — Stumpjumper headset/suspension problems](https://forums.mtbr.com/specialized/specialized-stumpjumper-headset-suspension-problems-1052081.html),

### Search 2: Specialized Stumpjumper review trail performance aluminum

- Alloy Stumpjumpers ride impressively well vs. comparably-priced competitors;
  same geometry and suspension tune as the carbon sibling.
- Weight penalty is real: alloy Stumpy tips ~36 lbs (vs. ~30 lbs carbon) —
  relevant for resale because weight-conscious buyers gravitate to carbon at
  comparable prices.
- Base alloy builds (SX Eagle, X-Fusion forks) exist and drag overall
  reputation; Comp Alloy (SLX/XT, Fox Rhythm or RockShox) is a meaningfully
  better product.
- Geometry is well-regarded: "longer, lower, slacker" treatment, S-Sizing.
- Sources: [BikeRadar — Stumpjumper Comp Alloy review](https://www.bikeradar.com/reviews/bikes/mountain-bikes/full-suspension-mountain-bikes/specialized-stumpjumper-comp-alloy-review),
