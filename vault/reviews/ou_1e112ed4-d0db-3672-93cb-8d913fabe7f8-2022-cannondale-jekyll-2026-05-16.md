## Web research

### Search 1: "2022 Cannondale Jekyll common issues reliability forum"
- The Jekyll's high-pivot design uses many pivots, making suspension overhaul expensive and parts-intensive. Multiple forum users note concern about long-term maintenance cost.
- RockShox Zeb Select (stock Jekyll 2 fork) is generally well-regarded for reliability; no specific defect reports.
- The Fox Float DPX2 proprietary shock design raised concern about parts availability and the need for a high-pressure pump for correct setup — not a defect, but a usability note.
- Rocks can find their way into the shock cavity due to the low-slung shock position — inspect for debris and seal damage.
- The WTB aluminum stock wheels (not present on this listing — already upgraded to ENVE) had spoke tension issues in reviews. The ENVE upgrade removes this known weakness.
- Cannondale's "AI Offset" wheel alignment (3mm non-drive-side dish offset) makes wheel compatibility slightly narrower — relevant when replacing tires or hubs.
